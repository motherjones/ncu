Subject line: The End of Meat?

This Week's Main Dish
We'll All Eat Less Meat Soon—Like It or Not
In a much-shared interview on the website of the Breakthrough Institute, Washington State University researcher Judith Capper champions the American system of raising cows on feedlots. "If we switched to all grass-fed beef in the United States, it would require an additional 64.6 million cows, 131 million acres more land, and 135 million more tonnes of greenhouse gas emissions," she said. "We'd have the same amount of beef, but with a huge environmental cost." Sounds reasonable enough—but there's a deeper question that Capper doesn't look at: Do we actually have enough resources to keep up the feedlot system?
