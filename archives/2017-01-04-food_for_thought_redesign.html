Tom's Breakfast of Champions

When recording Mother Jones' Bite podcast, we test our audio levels by telling each other what we had for breakfast that morning. It’s sort of a running joke that Tom Philpott always says the same thing:

"For I don't know how many months, this has been my standard breakfast. I keep waiting in vain to tire of it. It's important that the two even remotely fussy parts of this meal—the quinoa and the toasted nuts—be done in advance. If not, you're Doing It Wrong.

"So first, cook up a cup of quinoa, which will provide about a week's worth for one person. Store it in the fridge after it cools.

"To a medium-hot, dry skillet, add a mix of nuts, tossing them often so they don't scorch, until they smell nice and toasty. I use equal parts almonds, pumpkin seeds, and sesame seeds. When they are almost done, add some raw cacao nibs (in roughly the same portion as each of the nuts), and let them toast a bit. Let this nut-cacao mix cool, and then store it in a jar.

"When morning comes, add a modest portion of quinoa to a bowl. Top with a good dollop of whole-milk yogurt. Now add some fresh fruit (chopped apple, sliced banana, and berries all work) and some dried fruit (I've been using currants). Sprinkle on some of that nut-cacao mix, and add a light drizzle of maple syrup.

"When I'm feeling particularly gourmet, I finish with a light sprinkling of coarse sea salt. Mess it all up with a spoon, and then dig in."

That's all, folks! We'll be back next Wednesday with more.
—Maddie and Kiera
