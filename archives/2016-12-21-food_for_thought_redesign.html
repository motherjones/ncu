The Scary New Science That Shows Milk Is Bad for You

Contrary to what the mustachioed ads would have you believe, evidence suggests that dairy does not, in fact, do a body good. So why is the government still pushing three servings a day? (Mother Jones)

Look who's playing chicken with Donald Trump. President Barack Obama—whose administration still controls US food policy—just injected an extra dose of drama into Trump's chaotic transition. (Mother Jones)

"People eating their shrimp cocktails and paella may be getting more than they bargained for," says one infectious-disease expert—especially if the seafood's coming from China, where antibiotics are making their way through the food chain and into US imports. (Bloomberg Businessweek)

All kids see an increasing number of fast-food ads—but black kids see thousands more than their white counterparts, says a new study. (Vice)

A richly detailed, illustrated, and diagrammed guide to the food of the moment: the dumpling. (New York)

Most scientists think the seaweed-based food additive carrageenan is safe. But consumers are still scared of it, so companies are ditching the ingredient. (NPR)

This Week in Podcasts

"It's this idea of, 'If I tattoo my neck, if I tattoo my knuckles, I can't just walk away from this and start selling cars or sit in a cubicle. This is going to be my life.'"

That's BuzzFeed book editor Isaac Fitzgerald, explaining one of the reasons so many cooks decide to get tattoos. For their new illustrated book, Knives and Ink, he and co-author Wendy MacNaughton interviewed more than 65 chefs about the fascinating tales behind their body art. 

Hear about it on Bite podcast, episode 21:
"The Secret Lives of Chefs"

Puerto Ricans turn to coquito, a rich coconut eggnog-like beverage, and pitorro, a super strong (legal!) moonshine, to get their holiday parties started. Hear from the masters about making these drinks. (The Sporkful)

2016 could easily be deemed The Year of LaCroix (sparkling water, that is). What is fizzy water, anyway? Where did it come from—and is it good for you? (Gastropod)
