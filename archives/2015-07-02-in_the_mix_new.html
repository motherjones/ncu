It's Time for the Black Rights Movement to Finally Embrace Gay Rights
On social media, I've seen black people imply marriage equality is a frivolous concern, and that gay people shouldn't have received the right to marry before black people got the right to walk down the street without being shot by the police. I've seen black people argue against gay marriage by pointing out that it's still not legal to smoke weed. Then there are those who reject gay marriage and homosexuality as a sin. Though a growing number of people in the United States approve of same-sex marriage, only a minority of black Americans do, and support is less common among black Protestants than it is among all other regligious groups except white evangelicals. But the simple truth is this: It's problematic for members of any marginalized group to challenge the progress made by members of another, especially when both groups suffer as a result of the same system—a system that privileges whiteness, masculinity, heteronormativity, and cisgender status. And it's particularly problematic for black people to reject the LGBT struggle for equal rights.
