This Week in Podcasts

About 15.7 percent of US restaurant workers are undocumented immigrants, and another 5.9 percent are foreign-born US citizens. So when President Trump ramps up the pressure on undocumented US residents, he's also making life stressful for lots of the people who cook meals, wait tables, and wash dishes in this country. We talk to some restaurant owner/chefs about how they're pushing back.

Sanctuary Restaurants pledge not to "allow any harassment of any individual based on immigrant/refugee status, race, religion, gender, or sexual orientation to occur in their restaurant."

They also pledge to hang a "Sanctuary Restaurant" sign in their door. By last week, more than 100 eateries had signed on. Here are a few of the establishments across the country:
